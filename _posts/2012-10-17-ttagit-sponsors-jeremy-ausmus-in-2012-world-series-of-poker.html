Intigold Mines Ltd. - TTAGIT Sponsors Jeremy Ausmus in 2012 World Series of Poker

Vancouver, BC, October 17, 2012, Lori McClenahan, President and CEO of Intigold Mines Ltd. (SLI – TSX.V and IDMNF – OTC QX), announces that TTAGIT Social Networks Inc. ("TTAGIT") has sponsored Jeremy Ausmus in the 2012 World Series of Poker Main Event.  Intigold owns a 51% interest in TTAGIT.

TTAGIT has sponsored Jeremy Ausmus in the 2012 World Series of Poker.  Ausmus, a professional poker player and one of the nine players remaining at the 2012 World Series of Poker, has a chance to win over $8.5 million.  The 2012 World Series of Poker has brought him close to record-breaking notoriety, cashing in nine events and outlasting nearly 6,600 people to earn a seat at the final table where the winner will be decided on October 30, 2012.  ESPN will air the final table of the 2012 World Series of Poker “live” starting October 29, 2012.

“TTAGIT.com, a revolutionary leading-edge social media company, is ready to win the world over just like me in the world series of poker” stated Ausmus in a recent interview.

Jeremy Ausmus is represented by Lisa Tenner, President of Tenner and Associates, Inc., a Las Vegas-based branding and marketing company, focusing on talent building and brand-marketing skills in the corporate world.

TTAGIT, the first open-forum discussion social network and comment management system that permits users to comment on all URLs with one login, allows you to talk over any website without leaving the page.  TTAGIT is a social networking tool where members can engage in conversations and participate in debates on sites and portals that currently do not allow live streaming.  Contrary to most social media sites, TTAGIT is an open forum that is not just limited to the people you know or “follow.”  For more information please visit: http://ttagit.com/

Developed and owned 51% by Intigold Mines Ltd., TTAGIT is an application and website that is a valuable tool for everyone from sports fans, reporters, corporations, politicians to the general public. In addition, TTAGIT technologies are geared toward the rising mobility use and the upcoming Google TV.  There is also an advertising model built in to TTAGIT that lets clients post within a related comment feed.
